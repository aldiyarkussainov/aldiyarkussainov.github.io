02 · About

Most designers think like designers.

Biography

I got into design by accident — went to university for a degree, and design pulled me in. Over six years I've worked across different industries: from illustrations for Bilimland, Kazakhstan's largest education platform, to interfaces for the state media holding Qazcontent, and products at Ozimiz, an IT outsourcing company.

I also run operations as COO of the team in parallel.

I'm drawn to hard problems. Enterprise admin panels with dozens of tables, multi-role systems where one product serves the patient, another the doctor, and a third the administrator, products with high data density where the user has no time to figure things out.

I take such projects end-to-end — from the first conversation with the client to the final screens and post-launch support. A designer must listen to the client but think for the user — that's not a contradiction, that's the job.
