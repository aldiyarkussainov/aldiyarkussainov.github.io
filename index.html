Overview

A platform that connects patients, doctors, and universities

EMDEU is a digital telehealth integration platform designed for the CIS healthcare market. The product allows patients to view medical records, book appointments at both private and state clinics, and track health indicators — all in one place.

I was brought in to redesign the existing experience across three distinct products: the patient mobile app, the medical staff admin panel with a full ICD-10 patient registry, and a lightweight psychological testing tool for universities.

Problem

Three products, three different pain points

Problem 01 · Patient App

The existing app had poor UX flow with an outdated interface. The primary users are elderly patients — a demographic that requires simplified navigation and larger, clearer UI elements.

Problem 02 · Admin Panel

Doctors were working with a visually overwhelming interface. All 18 ICD-10 medical directions were scattered — there was no unified view, and navigation was exhausting for daily clinical work.

Problem 03 · University Tool

Three medical universities needed a psychological testing tool for students but couldn't afford custom development. They needed a simple, frictionless experience — no registration, no complexity, just guide the student from question A to result B.

Design Process

Key design decisions

Each product required a different design approach. Here are the decisions that mattered most:

  • 01

    Designing for elderly users first

    The primary patient audience is older adults. I simplified the navigation flow, increased touch targets, used clear iconography with labels, and kept the visual hierarchy calm and uncluttered. If an elderly user can do it easily, anyone can.

  • 02

    Accessibility built-in from day one

    In the admin panel, I added a dedicated accessibility mode for colorblind doctors and visually impaired medical staff — because it turned out some of EMDEU's actual users needed it. This wasn't an afterthought; it became a core feature.

  • 03

    Unified registry: 18 directions in one interface

    Instead of separate screens per ICD-10 category, I designed a single unified workspace where doctors can split or merge patient record pages. This dramatically reduced the number of clicks needed for daily clinical documentation.

  • 04

    Zero-friction university tool

    For the student testing app, no registration, no onboarding, no distractions. Open the app → answer the questionnaire → see the result. The entire UX was designed around removing every possible barrier between student and test completion.

Solution · Patient App

Clean, calm, and built for real people

The redesigned patient app focuses on clarity. The home screen gives a personalised health status at a glance, easy access to specialists by category, and one-tap entry to teleconsultation. Health trackers show data visually with historical trends. Appointment confirmation is minimal — just the essential information a patient needs before a call.

The waiting screen before a teleconsultation was designed to reduce anxiety — a timer, clear date and time, doctor's name, and a single action button. Nothing else. For patients calling a doctor remotely for the first time, this clarity is everything.

Result

Three products ready to launch across CIS

All three products are currently at the launch stage. EMDEU's platform will serve patients, medical professionals, and university students across Kazakhstan and broader CIS markets.

This project pushed me to think beyond screens — understanding clinical workflows, patient psychology, accessibility needs, and the constraints of underfunded university systems all shaped the final designs.
